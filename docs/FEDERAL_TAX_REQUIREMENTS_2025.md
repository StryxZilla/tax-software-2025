## 1. Income Sources

All US citizens and residents must report their worldwide income. The following types of income must be included on the tax return:

### 1.1 W-2 Wages (Form W-2)
- **Box 1:** Wages, tips, other compensation (taxable wages)
- **Box 2:** Federal income tax withheld
- **Box 3:** Social Security wages
- **Box 4:** Social Security tax withheld
- **Box 5:** Medicare wages and tips
- **Box 6:** Medicare tax withheld
- **Box 10:** Dependent care benefits
- **Box 11:** Nonqualified plans
- **Box 12:** Various compensation items (codes A-Y, etc.)
- **Box 13:** Statutory employee / Retirement plan / Third-party sick pay

### 1.2 1099 Forms (Various Types)

| Form | Purpose | Key Boxes |
|------|---------|-----------|
| **1099-NEC** | Nonemployee compensation | Box 1: Nonemployee compensation |
| **1099-K** | Payment card/Third-party network | Box 1a: Gross amount, Box 1b: Amount settled |
| **1099-MISC** | Miscellaneous income | Box 1: Rents, Box 2: Royalties, Box 3: Other income, Box 7: Nonemployee compensation |
| **1099-INT** | Interest income | Box 1: Interest income, Box 2: Early withdrawal penalty, Box 3: Interest on US Savings Bonds/Treasury obligations |
| **1099-DIV** | Dividends | Box 1a: Total ordinary dividends, Box 1b: Qualified dividends, Box 2a: Total capital gain distributions, Box 3: Nontaxable distributions |
| **1099-OID** | Original Issue Discount | Box 1: Original issue discount, Box 2: Other periodic receipts |
| **1099-B** | Proceeds from broker/barter exchanges | Box 1d: Proceeds, Box 2: Cost basis, Box 3: Gain/loss |
| **1099-S** | Real estate proceeds | Box 2: Gross amount paid |
| **1099-G** | Government payments | Box 1: State/local tax refund, Box 2: Unemployment compensation |
| **1099-R** | Distributions from retirement plans | Box 1: Gross distribution, Box 2a: Taxable amount |
| **1099-C** | Cancellation of debt | Box 2: Amount of debt canceled |

### 1.3 Capital Gains/Losses (Schedule D)
- **Short-term capital gains:** Assets held ≤1 year
- **Long-term capital gains:** Assets held >1 year
- **Reportable events:** Stock sales, real estate, cryptocurrency, mutual funds, business assets
- **Form 8949:** Sales and other dispositions of capital assets
- **Carryover:** Net capital losses can offset up to $3,000 of ordinary income per year; excess carries forward

### 1.4 Business Income (Schedule C - Profit or Loss from Business)
- Sole proprietorship income
- Single-member LLC income
- Independent contractor income (1099-NEC)
- Gig economy income
- **Expenses:** Advertising, car/truck, commissions, contract labor, depletion, employee benefits, insurance, interest, legal/professional, office expenses, rent/lease, repairs, supplies, taxes, travel, meals (50%), utilities, wages

### 1.5 Rental Income (Schedule E - Supplemental Income)
- Rental real estate
- Royalties
- REMIC residual interests
- **Expenses:** Advertising, auto/travel, cleaning, commissions, insurance, legal, management, mortgage interest, repairs, supplies, taxes, utilities

### 1.6 Farm Income (Schedule F - Profit or Loss from Farming)
- Farming operations
- **Expenses:** Car/truck, chemicals, conservation, custom hire, employee benefits, feed, fertilizer, freight, fuel, insurance, interest, labor, ladder, lubricants, management, pension, repairs, seeds, storage, supplies, taxes, utilities, veterinary

### 1.7 Retirement Income

| Source | Tax Treatment |
|--------|---------------|
| **Traditional IRA** | Taxed upon withdrawal |
| **Roth IRA** | Qualified distributions tax-free |
| **401(k)/403(b)** | Pre-tax contributions; taxed upon withdrawal |
| **Roth 401(k)/403(b)** | After-tax contributions; qualified withdrawals tax-free |
| **Pensions** | Taxed as ordinary income |
| **Social Security** | May be partially taxable based on combined income |

- **Form 1099-R:** Reports distributions from retirement accounts
- **Early withdrawal penalty:** 10% penalty if withdrawn before age 59½ (with exceptions)

### 1.8 Other Income

| Type | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Alimony** | For divorces finalized before 2019; taxable to recipient |
| **Unemployment compensation** | Fully taxable (1099-G) |
| **Gambling winnings** | Report as other income; losses deductible to extent of winnings |
| ** jury duty** | Included in gross income |
| **Hobby income** | Must be reported; expenses deductible only to extent of income |
| **Scholarships** | Taxable if for room/board; tax-free for qualified expenses |
| **Cancellation of debt** | Generally taxable income (1099-C) |
| **Tip income** | Must be reported; new deduction up to $25,000 for 2025-2028 |
| **Overtime pay** | New deduction up to $12,500 for 2025-2028 |

## 2. Above-the-Line Deductions

Above-the-line deductions reduce gross income before calculating adjusted gross income (AGI). They are available whether or not the taxpayer itemizes.

### 2.1 Traditional IRA Contributions
- **Maximum deduction (2025):** $7,500 ($8,600 if age 50+)
- **Phase-out if covered by workplace retirement plan:**
  - Single/HOH: $79,000 - $89,000 MAGI (full phase-out at $89,000+)
  - Married Joint: $126,000 - $146,000 MAGI (full phase-out at $146,000+)
  - Married Separate: $0 - $10,000 MAGI (full phase-out at $10,000+)
- Spouse not covered but taxpayer is: $236,000 - $246,000 MAGI

### 2.2 Student Loan Interest Deduction
- **Maximum deduction:** $2,500/year
- **Phase-out ranges (2025):**
  - Single/HOH/Qualifying Surviving Spouse: $85,000 - $100,000 MAGI
  - Married Filing Jointly: $170,000 - $200,000 MAGI
- **Note:** Married filing separately cannot claim this deduction

### 2.3 HSA Contributions (Health Savings Account)
- **Self-only coverage (2025):** $4,300 maximum
- **Family coverage (2025):** $8,550 maximum
- **Additional catch-up (age 55+):** $1,000
- **Must be covered by:** High-deductible health plan (HDHP)

### 2.4 Self-Employment Expenses
- **Self-employment tax deduction:** 50% of SE tax
- **Health insurance deduction:** Premiums for self, spouse, dependents
- **Retirement plans:** SEP-IRA, SIMPLE IRA, Solo 401(k) contributions
- **Home office:** Simplified method ($5/sq ft up to 300 sq ft) or regular method
- **Business expenses:** Deductible on Schedule C

### 2.5 Educator Expenses
- **Maximum deduction (2025):** $300 per educator
- **Eligible expenses:** Books, supplies, computer equipment, software, professional development
- **Eligibility:** K-12 teachers, instructors, counselors, principals

### 2.6 Other Above-the-Line Deductions

| Deduction | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| **Alimony paid** | For divorces finalized before 2019 |
| **IRA deduction** | See Section 2.1 |
| **Student loan interest** | See Section 2.2 |
| **HSA deduction** | See Section 2.3 |
| **Educator expenses** | See Section 2.5 |
| **SE health insurance** | Self-employed health insurance premiums |
| **SE retirement contributions** | SEP, SIMPLE, Solo 401(k) |
| **Jury duty pay** | If employer paid wages while on jury duty |
| **Moving expenses** | Active-duty military only |
| **Qualified business income (QBI)** | 20% deduction for pass-through businesses (Section 199A) |

### 2.7 NEW: Schedule 1-A Deductions (2025-2028)
The "One Big Beautiful Bill" added new deductions for 2025-2028:

| Deduction | Maximum | Phase-out |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Tip Income Deduction** | $25,000 | $25,000 - $50,000 (single); $50,000 - $75,000 (MFJ) |
| **Overtime Deduction** | $12,500 ($25,000 MFJ) | $75,000 - $100,000 (single); $150,000 - $200,000 (MFJ) |
| **Car Loan Interest** | $10,000 | $100,000 - $135,000 (single); $200,000 - $270,000 (MFJ) |
| **Senior Deduction (65+)** | $6,000 ($12,000 MFJ) | $75,000 - $100,000 (single); $150,000 - $200,000 (MFJ) |

## 3. Deductions

### 3.1 Standard Deduction (2025)

| Filing Status | Standard Deduction |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Single | $15,750 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $31,500 |
| Married Filing Separately | $15,750 |
| Head of Household | $23,625 |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse | $31,500 |

**Additional Standard Deduction (Age 65+/Blind):**

| Filing Status | Additional Amount |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Single | $2,000 ($4,000 if both 65+ and blind) |
| Head of Household | $2,000 ($4,000 if both 65+ and blind) |
| Married Filing Jointly | $1,600 per spouse ($3,200 if both 65+ and blind) |
| Married Filing Separately | $1,600 per spouse ($3,200 if both 65+ and blind) |

**Dependent Limitations:**
- Greater of: $1,350 OR ($450 + earned income, up to $15,750)

### 3.2 Itemized Deductions (Schedule A)

If itemized deductions exceed the standard deduction, taxpayers may itemize.

#### Medical and Dental Expenses
- Deductible to extent exceeding 7.5% of AGI
- Includes: Insurance premiums, doctors, dentists, hospitals, prescriptions, glasses, hearing aids, transportation for medical care

#### State and Local Taxes (SALT) - IMPORANT CHANGES FOR 2025
- **SALT Cap (2025):** $40,000 ($20,000 married filing separately)
- **New Phase-out:** If MAGI exceeds $500,000 ($250,000 MFJ), the cap is reduced by 30% of excess until it reaches $10,000
- Includes: State/local income taxes OR sales taxes, property taxes

#### Mortgage Interest
- **Acquisition debt:** Interest on up to $750,000 of debt ($375,000 MFS)
- **Home equity:** Interest deductible if loan secured by home (same limits apply)
- **Form 1098:** Mortgage interest statement from lender

#### Charitable Contributions
- **Cash contributions:** Up to 60% of AGI
- **Property contributions:** Up to 30% of AGI (50% for qualified organizations)
- **Required:** Written acknowledgment for donations $250+
- **Donor-advised funds:** Deductible but may have limitations

#### Casualty and Theft Losses
- Only presidentially declared disasters
- Deductible to extent exceeding 10% of AGI
- $100 per casualty floor

#### Miscellaneous Itemized Deductions (No longer deductible after 2027)
- Unreimbursed employee expenses
- Tax preparation fees
- Investment expenses
- Hobby expenses

### 4.2 Non-Refundable Credits

- **Required:** SSN valid for employment
- **Phase-out:** Begins at $200,000 MAGI ($400,000 MFJ)
- **Refundable portion:** Up to $1,700 per child (ACTC)
- **Other dependent credit:** $500 for dependents who don't qualify for CTC

## 5. Tax Calculations

### 5.1 Ordinary Income Tax Brackets (2025)

**Single / Married Filing Separately:**

| Rate | Income Range |
|------|-------------|

| 12% | $12,401 - $50,400 |

| 24% | $105,701 - $201,775 |
| 32% | $201,776 - $256,225 |
| 35% | $256,226 - $640,600 |
| 37% | Over $640,600 |

**Married Filing Jointly / Qualifying Surviving Spouse:**

**Head of Household:**

| 12% | $18,651 - $75,500 |

| 32% | $192,451 - $243,850 |
| 35% | $243,851 - $609,350 |

### 5.2 Capital Gains Rates (2025)

| Filing Status | 0% Rate | 15% Rate | 20% Rate |
|---------------|---------|----------|----------|
| Single | $0 - $51,250 | $51,251 - $518,900 | Over $518,900 |
| Married Joint | $0 - $102,500 | $102,501 - $583,750 | Over $583,750 |
| Head of Household | $0 - $77,200 | $77,201 - $551,350 | Over $551,350 |
| Married Separate | $0 - $51,250 | $51,251 - $291,850 | Over $291,850 |

**Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT):**
- 3.8% on lesser of net investment income OR amount by which MAGI exceeds:
  - $200,000 (Single)
  - $250,000 (Married Filing Jointly)
  - $125,000 (Married Filing Separately)

### 5.3 Self-Employment Tax (2025)

- **Social Security portion:** 12.4% on first $176,100 of net self-employment income
- **Medicare portion:** 2.9% on all net self-employment income
- **Additional Medicare Tax:** 0.9% on earnings over $200,000 (single) / $250,000 (married)
- **Total SE tax:** 15.3% (12.4% + 2.9%)
- **Deduction:** 50% of SE tax is deductible above-the-line

### 5.4 Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)

**AMT Exemption (2025):**
- Single: $85,700 (phase-out begins at $609,000)
- Married Filing Jointly: $133,300 (phase-out begins at $1,218,000)
- Married Filing Separately: $66,650 (phase-out begins at $609,000)

**AMT Rates:**
- 26% on first $232,600 of AMT income (single)
- 28% on AMT income above $232,600

### 5.5 Tax Computation Worksheet

```
Gross Income
- Above-the-line deductions (Schedule 1)
= Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)

- Greater of:
  - Standard Deduction, OR
  - Itemized Deductions (Schedule A)
= Taxable Income

Apply Tax Brackets:
- Ordinary Income → Regular tax rates
- Long-term Capital Gains → Preferential rates
- Qualified Dividends → Preferential rates

+ Net Investment Income Tax (if applicable)
+ Additional Medicare Tax (if applicable)
+ Alternative Minimum Tax (if applicable)
- Tax Credits
= Final Tax Liability
```

## 6. Filing Requirements

### 6.1 Who Must File (2025)

**General Rule - Income Thresholds:**

| Filing Status | Under 65 | 65 or Older |
|---------------|----------|-------------|
| Single | $15,750 | $17,550 |
| Head of Household | $23,625 | $25,625 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $31,500 | $33,100 (one 65+); $34,700 (both 65+) |
| Married Filing Separately | $5 | $5 |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse | $31,500 | $33,100 |

**Must File Regardless of Income:**
- Self-employment income over $400
- Taxable Social Security benefits (if in higher brackets)
- Household employees (if paid $2,600+)
- Taxable fringe benefits
- Individuals with over $5 in MFS itemizing while spouse doesn't

### 6.2 Filing Deadlines

| Event | Due Date |
|-------|----------|
| **Individual Tax Returns** | April 15, 2026 |
| **Self-Employment (Schedule C/F)** | April 15, 2026 |
| **Extension Deadline** | October 15, 2026 |
| **Foreign Account Reporting (FBAR)** | April 15, 2026 (auto-extends to Oct 15) |

**If April 15 Falls on Weekend/Holiday:**
- Next business day becomes due date

### 6.3 Extension Filing

- **Form 4868:** Application for Automatic Extension of Time to File
- **Extension:** 6 months (to October 15)
- **Payment:** Tax owed is still due April 15; can pay with extension
- **Electronic:** Can file extension online via IRS Direct Pay, credit card, or EFTPS

### 6.4 Filing Statuses

| Status | Requirements |
|--------|--------------|
| **Single** | Unmarried, legally separated, or divorced as of Dec 31 |
| **Married Filing Jointly** | Married, both agree to combine income/deductions |
| **Married Filing Separately** | Married, separate liability; may lose some credits |
| **Head of Household** | Unmarried, paid >50% household costs, dependent lived with you 6+ months |
| **Qualifying Surviving Spouse** | Spouse died in 2023 or 2024, dependent child, meet other tests |
| **Widows/Widowers** | Use qualifying surviving spouse or single |
